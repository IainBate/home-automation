How Off-Peak Charging Works Outside Standard Periods

Your system detects when the Ohme EV charger is drawing power. When this happens, the battery enters FORCE_CHARGE mode. The key question is: at what rate are you charged for this grid power?

The answer depends on WHEN the car charging starts:

  1. If car charges during off-peak (00:00-05:00): You pay GBP 0.07/kWh for battery charging + car power.
  2. If car charges during daytime/evening: You pay GBP 0.25/kWh - but your battery can help absorb some of this load!

The savings advantage comes from:

  • Using your battery to shift peak loads to cheaper periods
  • Battery smoothing reduces high-demand charges during expensive rates
  • Fully charging during cheapest rates (00:00-05:00) for use during expensive peak hours
  • When car is charging at off-peak, battery also charges cheaply, reducing grid import

Detecting car charging: We identify car charging periods by looking for sustained high load (> 5kW) over 10+ minutes. This is more reliable than relying solely on Ohme API integration.

Appendix: How the Analysis Works

This analysis compares three simulation approaches using your actual 5-minute interval data from the SolaX Cloud API:

  1. Standard Approach Simulation: Calculates what your costs would be without any battery control. PV powers your home directly, and any shortfall is covered by grid power at time-of-use rates.
  2. Current Algorithm Simulation: Uses your actual mode change log to reconstruct what the battery was doing at each 5-minute interval. The simulation:
    • Uses the recorded battery mode (SELF_USE, FORCE_CHARGE, FORCE_DISCHARGE)
    • Applies battery charge/discharge efficiency (92%)
    • Tracks state of charge throughout the period
    • Calculates grid power costs at the actual rate for each time period
  3. Proposed Algorithm Simulation: Simulates an idealized "perfect" strategy that:
    • Charges only during cheapest rates (00:00-05:00)
    • Discharges during peak rates (17:00-20:00) to cover household load
    • Maintains battery at optimal state of charge

Note on car charging detection: Since the cloud data doesn't include Ohme charging events, we infer car charging from sustained high load patterns. When load exceeds 5kW for more than 10 minutes, it's likely the EV charger is active.
